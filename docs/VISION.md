# Hablaba — Product Vision

## What We're Building

Hablaba is a Spanish language app for parents who want to raise bilingual children without following a strict "one parent, one language" rule. Instead, it supports a whole-home ambient immersion approach — Spanish woven into daily routines, repeated naturally, consistently, and confidently.

The app has two modes that work together:

- **Speak** — helps you speak Spanish *to your baby* in the right moments
- **Practice** — helps you improve *your own* Spanish through AI-guided conversation

The better you Practice, the more confidently you Speak.

---

## The Core Philosophy

Children acquire language through massive repetition of words attached to real-world context. A baby doesn't need a curriculum — they need to hear the same words, said by people they love, in the same situations, hundreds of times.

Parents don't need to be fluent to give their child a head start. They need:
1. The right words for the right moments
2. Consistent repetition
3. Enough confidence to actually say them

Hablaba provides all three.

## Speak Mode

Speak mode is a toolkit for parents. It is not a baby app — it helps the parent know what to say and when to say it.

### Phase 1: 0–6 Months (Immersion & Imprinting)

At this stage, babies are absorbing phonemes, prosody, and rhythm. Meaning comes later; exposure comes now. The goal is high-repetition, routine-attached phrases.

**Features:**
- **Routine Phrase Cards** — pre-built phrase sets for daily moments: diaper change, feeding, bath, bedtime, waking up, going outside. 3–5 phrases per routine, same words every time.
- **Daily Word** — one imperative or high-frequency word per day (e.g. *mira*, *ven*, *toma*, *espera*) with 3 example sentences showing how to use it naturally
- **Audio Playback** — hear any phrase spoken aloud before saying it, so pronunciation is consistent from day one (uses the existing TTS system)
- **Phrase Repetition Tracker** — mark which phrases you used today; see your weekly consistency

**Key principle:** Same words. Every time. Every routine.

### Phase 2: 6+ Months (Responsive Absorption)

Babies begin to understand language before they speak it. They start to respond to their name, familiar words, and simple commands. This is the window to build receptive vocabulary.

**Features:**
- **Imperative Library** — a curated set of commands the baby can learn to respond to: *dame*, *abre*, *cierra*, *ven aquí*, *siéntate*, *mira*, *más*, *ya*
- **Object Labeling Prompts** — when baby points or reaches, what to say. Organized by environment: bedroom, kitchen, outdoors, bath
- **Songs & Rhymes** — short, repetitive Spanish nursery rhymes and songs. Melodic content the baby begins to anticipate and recognize
- **Milestone Log** — mark the first time baby responds to a word. A record of early language acquisition

### Routine Builder

Parents can create custom routines and attach their own phrase sets. A routine has:
- A name and emoji
- A time of day (optional)
- A set of Spanish phrases (with audio)

This lets families build a phrase bank that fits their home, not a generic curriculum.

### Two-Parent Consistency

Both parents using the same words is critical. Without accounts or a database, consistency is supported by:
- A shared phrase bank visible to anyone using the app on the same device
- Future: exportable/shareable routine sets so both parents have the same list

## Practice Mode

Practice mode is the existing AI chat tutor. The user converses with a GPT-4o assistant in Spanish. The assistant:
- Responds naturally in Spanish at a B1 level
- Identifies and gently corrects grammar mistakes
- Keeps conversation going with topic prompts

Practice mode is for the parent's own development. As parents become more fluent and less self-conscious, they speak more Spanish at home — which is the whole point.

### Topic Areas Relevant to Parenting
In addition to general conversation topics, Practice mode should include topics that mirror real home life:
- *Talking about your day with a baby*
- *Describing what you're doing out loud*
- *Narrating a walk or meal*
- *Talking to your partner about the baby in Spanish*

## Guiding Principles

1. **Repetition over breadth** — fewer words, used more often, is better than a large vocabulary used once
2. **Routine-attached** — language sticks when it's tied to consistent physical contexts
3. **Parent confidence first** — a parent who feels awkward will go quiet; the app should reduce friction, not add it
4. **No guilt** — missing a day is fine; the app supports, not judges
5. **Ambient, not instructional** — this is not a class for the baby; it's a shift in how the home sounds

---

## What This Is Not

- Not a baby app (no screens for infants)
- Not a strict bilingual methodology enforcer
- Not a replacement for fluent input — but a bridge until parents get there
- Not dependent on both parents being equally committed

## Future Directions

- Native iOS/Android app for faster access in the moment (mid-diaper-change, nobody has time to open a browser)
- Partner sync — shared phrase sets and streak across two parent accounts
- Age-adaptive content — app adjusts phrase complexity and topics as baby grows
- Community phrase packs — families share routine sets that worked for them
