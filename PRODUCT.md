# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Parents raising bilingual (Spanish-speaking) babies. They are not language students in the traditional sense; they want to speak Spanish around the home naturally, without strict one-parent-one-language rules. They use the app in-the-moment during daily routines (feeding, bath time, play) and in short practice sessions when they have a few minutes. Most are B1-level Spanish speakers who need confidence and vocabulary, not grammar drills.

## Product Purpose

Hablaba makes daily Spanish practice feel easy and natural. Two modes serve the same parent: **Speak** gives them ready-to-use phrases for talking to their baby right now; **Practice** lets them chat with an AI tutor to build their own fluency. Success looks like a parent who reaches for Spanish instinctively during routines, not one who completes lessons.

## Brand Personality

Warm, calm, encouraging.

Like a patient friend who speaks Spanish with you over coffee. Never a teacher with a red pen, never a gamified app demanding streaks. The tone is gentle and supportive, celebrating small moments of progress. Think Headspace's clarity and calm crossed with Claude's friendly warmth.

## Anti-references

- **Duolingo**: Too informal, too gamified, too loud. The mascot-driven, streak-obsessed, notification-heavy approach is the opposite of what Hablaba should feel like.
- **Busuu**: Too functional and utilitarian. Feels like courseware, not a companion.
- **Generic language apps**: Flashcard grids, progress bars everywhere, achievement badges, competitive leaderboards.
- **Corporate SaaS**: Cold blues, dashboard-heavy layouts, data-dense screens.

## Design Principles

1. **Calm over clever**: Every screen should feel restful. No visual noise, no competing calls to action. If something can be removed, remove it.
2. **Warmth in the details**: Earthy tones, soft edges, gentle motion. The interface should feel like a warm room, not a productivity tool.
3. **Ready when you are**: The parent is holding a baby. Everything must be reachable in one hand, scannable in two seconds, useful in five.
4. **Encourage, never pressure**: No guilt for missed days. No streaks that punish. Progress is mentioned gently, never demanded.
5. **Show Spanish naturally**: Spanish should appear as a living language in context, not as isolated vocabulary items in boxes.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- WCAG 2.1 AA as baseline
- Touch targets minimum 44x44px (one-handed use with a baby in the other arm)
- Reduced motion support (respect prefers-reduced-motion)
- Sufficient color contrast on all earthy/warm tones (easy to under-contrast with light palettes)
- Audio content should have visual alternatives
